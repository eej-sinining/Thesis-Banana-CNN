Disease Reference

Understanding Banana Leaf Diseases

Learn about the diseases our AI can detect and recommended actions for each condition.

Healthy Leaf

A healthy banana leaf shows no signs of disease, with uniform green coloration and no spots or lesions.

Early Signs

  • → Uniform green color throughout
  • → No spots or lesions
  • → Smooth leaf surface
  • → Strong leaf structure

Suggested Actions

  • Continue regular care and maintenance
  • Monitor periodically for any changes
  • Maintain proper irrigation and nutrition

Cordana Leaf Spot

Cordana leaf spot is a fungal disease causing characteristic oval-shaped spots, typically appearing on older leaves first.

Early Signs

  • → Oval-shaped spots with gray centers
  • → Dark brown or purple borders around spots
  • → Usually appears on older leaves
  • → Spots may merge to form larger patches

Suggested Actions

  • Remove and destroy affected leaves
  • Improve air circulation around plants
  • Apply recommended fungicides if severe
  • Avoid overhead irrigation

Sigatoka Disease

Sigatoka is one of the most destructive diseases of bananas worldwide, significantly impacting yield and fruit quality.

Early Signs

  • → Yellow streaks parallel to leaf veins
  • → Streaks turn brown with gray centers
  • → Lesions spread and coalesce
  • → Premature leaf death in severe cases

Suggested Actions

  • Immediate removal of infected leaves
  • Apply protective and curative fungicides
  • Consider resistant varieties for replanting
  • Consult local agricultural extension services
  • Improve drainage and reduce humidity
